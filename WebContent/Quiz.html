Pregnancy Diet Quiz

Now that you're pregnant, you'll need more calcium, iron, fiber, protein, and other important nutrients for you and your growing baby. Whether you're hungry enough to literally "eat for two" or so queasy the mere thought of a meal is too much, healthy eating during pregnancy may not be as big a job as you think.

In fact, if you're starting pregnancy at a healthy weight, you need no additional calories in the first trimester, about 300 additional calories a day in the second trimester, and about 450 additional calories in the third. If you're overweight or underweight, you'll need more or less than this depending on your weight gain goal.

So every food choice counts! A kitchen stocked with plenty of healthy, filling options can help you quell your hunger pangs. Are your fridge, freezer, and pantry up to the challenge? Take the quiz and find out.
